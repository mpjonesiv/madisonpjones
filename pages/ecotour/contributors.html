Project Team

Developed by Shannon Butts, Jason Crider, Jacob Greene, and Madison Jones

Shannon Butts is a PhD candidate in English at the University of Florida. Her research examines how emerging writing technologies, such as augmented reality and 3D printing, can shape community literacy practices and mobilize local discourse. Follow her on Twitter @shannonbmarie.

Jason Crider is a Graduate Research Fellow in the Department of English at the University of Florida, where he researches and teaches at the intersection of writings studies and digital rhetoric. His research centers around multimodal composition, haptic and location-based writing technologies, and environmental rhetorics.

Jacob Greene is a recent graduate of the PhD in English program at the University of Florida, and his research focuses on emerging modalities of location-based writing, including augmented reality and locative media. Jacob will be joining the Writing, Rhetorics, and Literacies program at Arizona State University as an Assistant Professor in fall 2018.

Madison Jones is a PhD candidate in English at the University of Florida. His research explores digital ecologies and emerging technologies through place-based methodologies. Visit his website, or follow him on Twitter @poetrhetor.
