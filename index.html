About

I am a Doctoral Candidate and Graduate Research Fellow in the Department of English at the University of Florida, specializing in Rhetoric and Writing Studies with a secondary focus in Technical & Professional Communication. My dissertation project, Writing, Place, Network was awarded the Fall 2019 Graduate School Doctoral Dissertation Award. In the project, I use an ecological methodology to understand the place-based affordances of digital writing technologies. At UF, I teach courses in digital rhetoric, writing/argumentation, and technical/professional communication.

My research projects focus on the use of digital technologies for environmental communication, digital advocacy, and user-centered information design. Specifically, I examine how digital and visual design can combat the rhetorical problem of scale for communicating environmental problems. An important feature of all locative digital technologies, scale also detaches users from a sense of individual autonomy, such as in visualizations of climate change or sea-level rise, creating a disconnect between local action and global impact. By emphasizing the place-based affordances of mobile media, my research and digital projects demonstrate how emerging technologies like augmented reality can be used to combat the problem of scale in environmental communication and to promote advocacy within local communities. My articles have appeared in Enculturation, Rhetoric Review, Computers and Composition Online, Kairos, and elsewhere.

In addition to my research and teaching, I also serve as an Augmented Reality Criticisms (or ARCs) Coordinator for the Trace Innovation Lab, a digital rhetoric initiative at the University of Florida, through which I consult with faculty and graduate students in the development of digital projects, organize digital humanities conferences, and coordinate workshops geared toward undergraduate research.

Research & Teaching Areas

Digital rhetoric draws from rhetorical theory to understand the suasive elements, affordances, and limitations of digital media. Because rhetoric is both analytical and generative, digital rhetoric works within the possibilities of new media genres to understand and mobilize emerging technologies of writing.

  • Digital Advocacy
  • Visual & Digital Rhetorics
  • Mobile Media & Web Design
  • Circulation Studies
  • New Media Storytelling

Writing Studies is informed by interdiciplinary approaches to composition, creative writing, publishing studies, and multimodal information design to explore the ways that writing is generated, revised, and circulated. Core concerns include creativity and invention, grammar and style, argumentation and research, as well as the ways that digital publishing has transformed writing.

  • Multimodal Composition
  • Ecocomposition
  • Place-based Composition
  • Creative Writing Studies
  • Civic & Community Writing

Professional communication is a broad field of communication studies which focuses on how information exchanges take place across fields (such as a scientist communicating with the public), organizations (such as a business advertising to a target audience), and modalities (such as a customer interacting with a website).

  • Public Writing
  • Podcasting & Augmented Reality
  • Editing, Coding, & UI Design
  • Usability & UX Design
  • Science Writing
